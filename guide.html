ESFA Funded Adult Skills

Let's break down adult skills funding (ASF) and what it means for you. The government helps adults gain valuable qualifications through full funding, meaning you might be able to study for free! Essentially, the government wants to help adults improve their skills. There are several ways you can get funding, so it's worth exploring your options. Don't hesitate to ask for help from a training provider or careers advisor to find the best path for you. There are some key areas where you could get full funding:

  • English and Maths: If you're 19 or older and haven't achieved a GCSE grade A* to C (or grade 4 or above) in English or maths, you can study these subjects up to Level 2 for free. Even if you have GCSEs but your skills are below a Grade 4, you can also access this funding.
  • First Level 2 Qualification (Aged 19-23): If you're between 19 and 23 and this is your first full Level 2 qualification, it's likely to be fully funded.
  • First Level 3 Qualification (Aged 19-23): Similarly, if you're 19 to 23 and this is your first full Level 3 qualification, it should also be fully funded.
  • Digital Skills: If you're 19 or older and your digital skills are below Level 1, you can improve them for free with Essential Digital Skills Qualifications (EDSQs) or Digital Functional Skills qualifications (DFSQs) up to Level 1.
  • Other Opportunities: Depending on your situation (like being unemployed or earning below a certain amount), you might also be able to get full funding for courses that help you get a job, local courses, HGV licences, work placements, and some programs like SWAPs and The Prince's Trust. ESOL (English for Speakers of Other Languages) courses may also be fully funded.

Important Things to Know:

  • Only one at a time: If you're 19 or older and haven't achieved a GCSE grade A* to C (or grade 4 or above) in English or maths, you can study these subjects up to Level 2 for free. Even if you have GCSEs but your skills are below a Grade 4, you can also access this funding.
  • Talk to someone: Because everyone's situation is different, it's really important to get advice from a training provider or careers advisor. They can tell you exactly what funding you're eligible for and what courses are available. They can also help you plan your next steps after you finish your qualification.
  • Tailored Learning: There's also extra funding available for more flexible learning options. Ask your training provider for more details about this.

Good news! The government can help you learn new skills for free. This funding is for adults aged 19 and over, and it can help you get qualifications up to Level 2.

Who Qualifies for Full Funding?

  • Live in the UK (meet the residency rules)
  • Are 19 or older
  • Either earn less than a certain amount (below the earnings threshold) or are unemployed.

What Courses Are Covered?

  • Courses from the "local flexibility offer" (ask your local college or training provider about these).
  • Courses on the "full Level 2 legal entitlement qualification list" (this is a list of specific qualifications the government prioritises).

Special Rule for Younger Learners (19-23):

If you're between 19 and 23 and haven't already got a full Level 2 qualification, you can get a Level 2 course from the "legal entitlement list" fully funded, *even if* you earn more than the threshold *or* are employed. You won't have to pay any course fees.

What Happens if I Already Have a Level 2 Qualification?

If you've already got a full Level 2 qualification and you're not earning below the threshold or unemployed, you might still be able to get help with funding, but it will be "co-funded," meaning you'll likely have to pay some of the costs.

Where Can I Find the Approved Courses?

For the 2024-2025 funding year, you can find the approved courses on the government's website (it's called the "DfE list of qualifications approved for funding"). Ask your local college or training provider, and they can help you find it. They'll also be able to help you find out which courses are on the "local flexibility offer" and the "legal entitlement list."

Great news! If you're looking to gain higher-level skills, the government can help. This funding is available for adults, and it can help you get qualifications at Level 3.

Who Qualifies for Full Funding (Aged 19-23)?

  • If you're between 19 and 23 and haven't yet achieved a full Level 3 qualification, you can get a Level 3 course fully funded, no matter how much you earn or whether you're employed.
  • This applies if you choose a qualification from the "Level 3 legal entitlement list" or the "Free Courses for Jobs (FCFJ) list".

Where Can I Find the Approved Courses?

For the 2024-2025 funding year, you can find the approved courses on the government's website (it's called the "DfE list of qualifications approved for funding"). Ask your local college or training provider, and they can help you find it. They'll also be able to tell you which courses are on the "legal entitlement list" and the "FCFJ list".

What Happens if I've Already Got a Level 3 Qualification?

If you've already got a full Level 3 qualification and you don't meet the criteria (earnings or employment), you might still be able to get funding through an Advanced Learner Loan. This is a loan specifically for higher-level courses, and you'll need to pay it back after you finish your course and start earning above a certain amount. Your college or training provider can give you more information about Advanced Learner Loans.

Free Courses for Jobs (FCFJ) is a special programme at Level 3 designed to help adults who are either earning below a certain amount or are unemployed.

What's Included?

  • Level 3 qualifications that will help you develop new skills and improve your job prospects.
  • Eligible adults can now access fully-funded Level 3 courses from the list of approved FCFJ qualifications (available on the DfE list of qualifications approved for funding).

Funding for Younger Learners (19-23)

If you're aged 19 to 23 and eligible, your FCFJ course will be funded through your Adult Skills Fund (ASF) allocation, including the extra funding boost.

Approved Qualifications

Only qualifications on the approved FCFJ list will receive the extra funding. The list might change as new courses are added, so it's a good idea to check the list regularly.

Who Qualifies for Full Funding?

  • Aged 19 or above (by 31st August 2024/25): You must be 19 or older, enrol on an approved FCFJ qualification, and be either earning below the threshold or unemployed.
  • Aged 19 to 23 (by 31st August 2024/25): You must be 19 to 23, enrol on an approved FCFJ qualification, not have a full Level 3 qualification already, and earn *above* the earnings threshold.

Important Note:

You can't get funding from the Adult Skills Fund (ASF) for an FCFJ course if you're already getting funding for it through an Advanced Learner Loan or a Skills Bootcamp. Check the Advanced Learner Loans funding rules for more information about those loans.

English and Maths Courses

The government fully fund eligible learners for the following English and maths qualifications:

  • GCSE English and/or maths
  • Functional Skills English and/or maths (from Entry Level to Level 2)
  • "Stepping stone" qualifications (including parts of qualifications) in English and/or maths approved by the DfE

Who Qualifies for Free English and Maths Courses?

To be eligible, you must:

  • Live in the UK (meet the residency rules)
  • Be aged 19 or over
  • Not have a GCSE in English or maths at grade 4 (C) or above (or a qualification at the same level or higher), *or* have skills below a Grade 4 level (even if you've taken a GCSE or similar before).

Important Information:

  • If you want to retake a GCSE English or maths exam because you didn't get a grade 4 (C) or above, the government won't fund *just* the exam retake. They can fund the course to *prepare* for the retake.
  • Apprentices can't get English and/or maths funding through the Adult Skills Fund (ASF).
  • You can only enrol on qualifications that will help you progress towards a GCSE or Functional Skills Level 2.

Digital Skills Courses

The government will fully fund eligible learners, including those who are employed, for the following digital skills qualifications:

  • Essential Digital Skills Qualifications (EDSQ) up to and including Level 1
  • Digital Functional Skills Qualifications (DFSQ) up to and including Level 1

Who Qualifies for Free Digital Skills Courses?

To be eligible, you must:

  • Live in the UK (meet the residency rules)
  • Be aged 19 or over
  • Have digital skills below Level 1.

Eligibility and Residency

Earnings Threshold

The ESFA Adult Skills Fund (ASF) often uses an earnings threshold to determine eligibility for full funding of £25,000 per annum. This threshold is a specific income level, and learners earning below it may qualify for fully funded courses. The exact threshold amount varies and is subject to change, so it's crucial to check the latest government guidance or consult with a training provider for the most up-to-date figures. Meeting the earnings threshold is just one of several criteria, and other factors, such as age and prior qualifications, also play a role.

Employment Status

Employment status is a key factor in determining eligibility for ESFA Adult Skills Fund (ASF) funding. While some fully funded courses are available regardless of employment status (especially for learners aged 19-23 pursuing their first Level 2 or 3 qualification), many courses target individuals who are unemployed or facing redundancy. The specific eligibility criteria regarding employment status can vary depending on the course and funding stream, so it's best to speak with a training provider to understand the requirements for a particular course.

Residency

To be eligible for ESFA Adult Skills Fund (ASF) funding, learners must meet UK residency requirements. Generally, this means being ordinarily resident in the UK on the first day of learning. There are specific rules and exceptions, so it's essential to check the detailed residency criteria on the government website or speak to a training provider for individual circumstances. Residency requirements may vary depending on nationality, immigration status, and length of stay in the UK.
